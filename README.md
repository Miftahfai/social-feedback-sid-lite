# Social Feedback SID-Lite

A PsychoPy-coded, EEG-friendly social incentive delay task with social-media-style self and other posts.

## Current design

- Self posts use participant nickname + short typed self-post + chosen avatar
- Other posts use standardized usernames + standardized short text + avatars
- Reward and punishment cue conditions
- Adaptive RT thresholding targeting roughly 60% wins

## Main file

- `social_feedback_sid_lite.py`

## Notes

This task is a simplified EEG-compatible social feedback paradigm informed by multiple prior social and incentive delay task designs, including:

- Spreckelmeyer, K. N., Krach, S., Kohls, G., Rademacher, L., Irmak, A., Konrad, K., Kircher, T., & Gründer, G. (2009). *Anticipation of monetary and social reward differently activates mesolimbic brain structures in men and women*. Social Cognitive and Affective Neuroscience, 4(2), 158–165.
- Ait Oumeziane, B., Schryer-Praga, J., & Foti, D. (2017). *“Why don't they ‘like’ me more?”: Comparing the time courses of social and monetary reward processing*. Neuropsychologia, 107, 48–59.
- Nicolaou, S., Vega, D., & Marco-Pallarés, J. (2025). *Opening the Pandora box: Neural processing of self-relevant negative social information*. Biological Psychology, 194, 108982.
- Nicolaou, S., Julià, A., Otero, D., et al. (2025). *Reward-related neural activation during social media exposure in young women with non-suicidal self-injury: evidence for a continuum of severity in the reward network*. Translational Psychiatry, 15, 308.

This repository combines elements inspired by prior SID and social feedback paradigms, but does not reproduce any published task in full.
